The Problem

Gauteng moves
millions of people.
Nobody knew how.

Gauteng is Africa's most economically active province — a sprawling urban region where millions of commuters navigate a complex web of trains, buses, BRT systems and minibus taxis every single day. But for most of those commuters, the experience of public transport was defined by uncertainty: uncertain times, uncertain fares, uncertain connections, and no reliable way to plan a multi-modal journey from a single source.

The Gautrain Management Agency recognised that smart technology was the answer — a platform that brought the full complexity of Gauteng's transport network into a single, accessible, real-time interface for every commuter in the province. They chose Konecta Group to build it.

Problem 01
No single source of transport truth
Nine separate operators, each with their own timetables, fare structures and service updates — and no platform that consolidated them. Commuters checked multiple sources, made guesses and absorbed the cost of uncertainty in lost time and money every day.
Problem 02
Minibus taxis had no digital presence
The minibus taxi industry carries the majority of Gauteng's daily commuters — yet had no real-time digital integration with any journey planner. Building a solution that meaningfully included taxis was one of the most complex technical requirements of the project.
Problem 03
Cost and route optimisation was guesswork
Commuters — especially lower-income households making multiple transfers — had no way to identify the cheapest or fastest route across modes. The economic cost of poor route information fell hardest on those least able to absorb it.
"The Gauteng on the Move app fits into the province's modernisation agenda — providing commuters with real-time and point-to-point information when moving from a current to a desired location. There is no doubt that global technological changes are sparking a revolution in the transport sector, and we must adopt and adapt."
MEC Ismail Vadi · Gauteng Department of Roads & Transport · Launch Address
What Konecta Built

A province in
your pocket.

Building Gauteng on the Move required integrating real-time data feeds from nine separate operators — each with different data formats, update frequencies and technical architectures. Konecta designed and developed the full application from scratch, handling data integration, UI/UX, journey planning algorithms and both iOS and Android builds.

RT
Real-Time Transport Information
Live tracking and up-to-date service information across all nine operators — Gautrain, Metrobus, Rea Vaya, A Re Yeng, Metrorail, Gautrain Bus Services, Tshwane Bus Services, Johannesburg City Sightseeing and minibus taxi services.
JP
Multi-Modal Journey Planning
Point-to-point journey planning across all transport modes — with the ability to select preferred modes, travel times, and optimise for either fastest or cheapest route. Every combination of Gauteng's transport network in one interface.
FT
Live Timetables & Fares
Up-to-date timetables and fare information for all operators, surfaced contextually within the journey planner. Commuters could see exactly what a journey would cost before they started — a first for Gauteng's multi-modal system.
OS
iOS & Android — Full Dual Build
Konecta delivered native applications for both platforms, published to the Apple App Store and Google Play Store as a free download. Full quality assurance, app store optimisation and launch-day deployment managed end-to-end.

The Launch

Built, published
and launched.

Konecta's responsibility did not end at development. We managed the full go-to-market — from App Store and Google Play publication through to the public launch event and social media campaign that introduced Gauteng on the Move to the province.

Deliverable 01
App Store & Google Play Publication
Full end-to-end management of the iOS App Store and Google Play Store submission, review and publication process — including app store listing optimisation, screenshots, descriptions and launch-day deployment coordination.
Deliverable 02
Government Launch Event
The app was officially launched by MEC Ismail Vadi at the offices of T-Systems, in the presence of industry leaders, transport sector stakeholders and media. Konecta coordinated the technical demonstration and product showcase for the launch audience.
Deliverable 03
Social Media Launch Campaign
Konecta managed the social media rollout of Gauteng on the Move — building awareness among Gauteng's commuter population through a coordinated digital campaign that drove downloads across both platforms at launch.
