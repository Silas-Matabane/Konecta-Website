The Challenge

Free WiFi.
Wrong behaviour.

When Konecta deployed free community WiFi in Alexandra, the connectivity worked. People connected. Data flowed. But something wasn't right. The usage patterns told a story that the signal strength didn't: people were spending their free connectivity on social media and entertainment — not on the opportunities that could genuinely change their lives.

The infrastructure was delivering. The community wasn't getting the value it deserved from it. The question became: is this a connectivity problem, or a communication problem?

It was a communication problem. And that insight changed everything.

01
Connectivity without purpose
WiFi was deployed and publicised. Uptake was good. But usage analysis showed the community wasn't leveraging connectivity for economic or educational advancement — the opportunity wasn't being communicated.
02
Free misread as trivial
When something is free, its value is easily underestimated. Community members weren't connecting the free WiFi to real opportunity — job searches, online courses, assignments, research — because no one had made that connection explicit for them.
03
Communication gap, not skills gap
This was not a digital literacy problem. People knew how to use their phones. They needed someone to meet them halfway — to say: this connection is for your future, not just your feed.
"Communication is the one thing we take for granted and overlook in community network deployment. But it is the one thing that can change everything. Once people are aware of the opportunities — it triggers something."
Insight from Konecta's Alexandra Community WiFi Deployment
The Intervention

We changed
the message.

The technical infrastructure stayed the same. The signal, the bandwidth, the coverage — unchanged. What Konecta changed was the communication: the posters, the language, the framing. Instead of announcing that free WiFi existed, the new messaging told the community what free WiFi was for. The shift was immediate and measurable.

Phase 1 · Initial Deployment
Before
Free WiFi
is available here.
Standard connectivity announcement. Told the community the WiFi existed. Did not tell them what to do with it, why it mattered, or what it could unlock for them. The community connected — and defaulted to familiar behaviours.
↳ Result: High connection rates. Low productive use.
Phase 2 · Communication Pivot
After
"We want you
to do well..."
The new messaging spoke directly to aspiration: search for that job. Do that online course. Finish that assignment. Do that research. Chat with your loved ones. It named the specific uses — and made the implicit value of free connectivity explicit. Somebody was meeting the community halfway.
↳ Result: Measurable shift in usage toward productive activities.
The Taxi Rank Activation
"Taxi Fare is on YOU. WiFi is on US."
At the Alexandra taxi rank, Konecta deployed a second campaign that met commuters precisely where they were: about to spend money on transport. The message acknowledged their reality — the fare is yours to pay — and offered something back. It reframed free WiFi as a genuine exchange of value, not a handout. The tone of reciprocity changed the community's relationship with the service entirely.
The Intelligence Layer

WiFi as a
data tool.

Once communities were engaged and using the WiFi platform productively, Konecta deployed the next layer of the platform's capability: community intelligence gathering. At the Alexandra taxi rank, commuters were invited to participate in a short quiz through the WiFi portal.

The questions were simple and purposeful: why are you travelling today? Are you employed? Where are you going? The responses, aggregated across thousands of daily commuters, produced something that no government planning document had: ground-truth data on how township residents actually move, why they move, and what they need.

This data was compiled and shared with the Department of Transport — providing insights that can directly inform infrastructure planning, skills programme placement, employment activation targeting and public transport investment decisions.

DoT
Department of Transport Partnership
Konecta's commuter intelligence data was formally shared with the Department of Transport — providing real-time insights into commuter purpose, employment status and travel patterns that standard planning processes cannot produce.

Travel Pattern Mapping
By aggregating connection data across the taxi rank network, Konecta is able to map commuter flows, peak travel times and route patterns — intelligence that is directly applicable to transport network planning and investment prioritisation.

Understanding the percentage of commuters who are unemployed, seeking work, in training or employed provides government skills programmes with precise targeting data — so interventions reach the people who need them, at the location where those people already are.

What This Proves

Three lessons every
community network
must apply.

The Alexandra deployment is not just a project story. It is a methodology — one that is directly replicable in every township and peri-urban community where connectivity has been deployed without purpose, without community voice, and without an intelligence layer. These lessons apply to every municipality, DFI and private sector player investing in community connectivity.

Lesson 01 · Communication
Tell people what connectivity is for, not just that it exists
Deploying WiFi without behaviour change communication is deploying infrastructure without purpose. The message must name the specific opportunity — the job search, the online course, the assignment — and it must meet the community in their language, their context, their reality. Communication is not marketing. It is activation.
Lesson 02 · Intelligence
Every connection is a data point. Use it deliberately.
A community WiFi platform that only delivers internet access is using 20% of its potential. The portal is a direct, consensual channel to the community — for surveys, for needs assessment, for employment status tracking, for programme awareness. The data generated has value to government, to funders and to the community itself.
Lesson 03 · Value Exchange
Free only works when the community understands the exchange
Free connectivity perceived as trivial is wasted connectivity. When the community understands that someone is investing in their access — and that the expectation is that they use it to do well — the relationship changes. Reciprocity, even when the exchange is asymmetric, drives better outcomes than pure charity framing every time.
